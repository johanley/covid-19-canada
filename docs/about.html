Generalities about the data

  • start-date is 2020-03-31
  • updated daily, at about 21h ADT (after BC updates)
  • resides on this github repo
  • backed by screenshots of government webpages. This lets anyone verify the data.
  • available as both csv and json files

There are 3 data sets, for each of the 14 jurisdictions (10 provinces, 3 territories, and the federal government):

  • total reported deaths to date (cumulative).
  • total reported cases to date (cumulative). Confirmed cases plus probable/presumptive cases. Only confirmed cases have a positive test result.
  • total completed tests (cumulative). Excludes pending tests. (Not reported by the federal government.)

Defects in the data

Not all jurisdictions update their stats every day: when that happens, it's recorded as N. It will show up as an empty gap in time-series charts.

Not in sync: because of timing issues, it's common for the federal government's reporting of deaths and cases to not add up to corresponding numbers from the provinces and territories.

No test-data from the CA jurisdiction: the federal government reports on deaths and cases, but not on tests.

Reporting delays: sometimes the reporting isn't very timely. For example, if the ultimate source of the data is non-governmental (maybe a private long-term care home), it likely takes time for that data to get into government databases.

Reported cases versus actual cases: the number of cases reported should be treated with care. Not everyone is being tested, and all tests have false-negatives and false-positives. The number of actual cases is usually larger than the number reported.

Tests-performed versus people-tested: people are often tested more than once. Most jurisdictions report on the tests-performed, broken down into positive and negative results. NL and NU report instead on the number of people tested, which is of course a different statistic. In spite of this difference, the NL and NU data is here treated the same as the other jurisdictions.

Backfill for NB tests, 2020-03-31 to 2020-04-16 inclusive: an error was made in gathering this data. It has been back-filled using data from this source, and from virihealth.com. This is the only case in which the data is not backed up by a screenshot of a government website.
